Information and History

Sequoia National Park is one of the earliest created parks in the nation, established in 1890. The park spans 404,064 acres. The park is contiguous with Kings Canyon National Park and the two parks are administered by the National Park Sevice together as the Sequoia and Kings Canyon National Parks. The park is famous for several features including Mount Whitney, the tallest peak in the lower 48 states at 14,505 feet above sea level and its giant sequoia trees located in The Giant Forest, which contains five out of ten of the largest trees in the world.

Backcountry Geography

The vast majority of Sequoia National Park is roadless wilderness; no road crosses the Sierra Nevada within the park's boundries. Over eighty percent of Sequoia and Kings Canyon National Parks is designated wilderness and is accessible only by foot or horseback. Sequoia's backcounty offers a vast expanse of high alpine wonders that covers the highest-elevation region of the High Sierra accessible from the Giant Forest via the High Sierra Trail.
